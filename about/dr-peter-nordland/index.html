Biography

Periodontist & Esthetics Researcher

Dr. Peter Nordland is one of the most widely cited periodontists in the United States when it comes to the esthetics of gum tissue and tooth relationships. His landmark classification system for interdental papilla loss — published in the Journal of Periodontology — is now standard teaching in periodontal residency programs worldwide, and it has been referenced in hundreds of subsequent peer-reviewed studies.

Dr. Nordland completed his dental training at UCLA School of Dentistry, where he also earned his Certificate in Periodontics. He went on to become a diplomate of the American Board of Periodontology and a Fellow of the American Academy of Esthetic Dentistry — credentials that reflect both his clinical rigor and his commitment to the intersection of function and appearance.

In addition to his research contributions, Dr. Nordland has lectured extensively at national and international dental conferences, authored chapters in periodontal textbooks, and served as a mentor to residents and practicing periodontists. His presentations on root coverage, papilla reconstruction, and tissue management for implant esthetics are consistently among the most attended at major symposia.

In practice, Dr. Nordland is known for his meticulous attention to soft tissue architecture — the contours, symmetry, and color of gum tissue that separate a functional result from a beautiful one. His patients frequently remark that they never expected their periodontal outcome to look so natural.
